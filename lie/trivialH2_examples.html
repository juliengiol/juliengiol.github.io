Section 3.3 Examples

Â¶

We will now test our algorithm on some specific finite-dimensional nilpotent Lie algebras. Here it is in one piece, together with scNLA.

First, we take three examples in the article Adjoint and Trivial Cohomologies of Nilpotent Complex Lie Algebras of Dimension \(\leq 7\) by Louis Magnin. For each of these examples, we will print out a basis of

\begin{equation*} H^2(\mathfrak{g},\mathbb{C}) \end{equation*}

and check that its dimension is equal to Betti number \(b_2\text{.}\)

  1. Let us start with the \(7\)-dimensional NLA

    \begin{equation*} \mathfrak{g}_{7,1.7} \end{equation*}

    whose multiplication table is summarized by

    \begin{equation*} 12|4,\,13|5,\,14|6,\,15|7,\,23|6,\,24|7 \end{equation*}

    and whose Betti number \(b_2\) is equal to

    \begin{equation*} 7. \end{equation*}

  2. Then let us consider the \(7\)-dimensional NLA

    \begin{equation*} \mathfrak{g}_{7,2.27} \end{equation*}

    whose multiplication table is summarized by

    \begin{equation*} 12|5,\,13|7,\,15|6,\,24|7,\,25|7 \end{equation*}

    and whose Betti number \(b_2\) is equal to

    \begin{equation*} 8. \end{equation*}

  3. Finally let us consider the \(7\)-dimensional NLA

    \begin{equation*} \mathfrak{g}_{7,3.9} \end{equation*}

    whose multiplication table is summarized by

    \begin{equation*} 12|5,\,15|-7,\,23|6,\,24|7 \end{equation*}

    and whose Betti number \(b_2\) is equal to

    \begin{equation*} 9. \end{equation*}

  4. Now let us play with the NLAs of the array scNLA.

    First, let us compare the Betti numbers that we obtain here in trivial cohomology with those obtained in the previous chapter in homology.

    They are the same, i.e.

    \begin{equation*} \mathrm{dim}\,H^p(\mathfrak{g},\mathbb{C})=\mathrm{dim}\,H_p(\mathfrak{g},\mathbb{C})\quad\text{for all}\quad 0\leq p\leq\mathrm{dim}\,\mathfrak{g} \end{equation*}

    for all the NLAs of scNLA.

  5. Now let us take some 5-dimensional examples in the book Adjoint and Trivial Cohomology Tables for Indecomposable Nilpotent Lie Algebras of Dimension \(\leq 7\) over \(\mathbb{C}\) by Magnin, namely

    \begin{equation*} \mathcal{G}_{5,6}=\mathrm{scNLA}[5, 6],\;\mathcal{G}_{5,4}\dagger=\mathrm{scNLA}[5, 9],\;\mathcal{G}_{5,5}\dagger=\mathrm{scNLA}[5, 7],\;\text{and}\;\mathcal{G}_{5,2}\dagger=\mathrm{scNLA}[5, 8]. \end{equation*}

    For each of these algebras, we will print out the list of all dimensions of cocycle spaces

    \begin{equation*} \mathrm{dim}\,Z^p(\mathfrak{g},\mathbb{C})\quad\text{for}\quad 0\leq p\leq\mathrm{dim}\,5 \end{equation*}

    as well as the list of cohomology dimensions

    \begin{equation*} \mathrm{dim}\,H^p(\mathfrak{g},\mathbb{C})\quad\text{for}\quad 0\leq p\leq\mathrm{dim}\,5 \end{equation*}

    in order to compare them with those furnished by Magin.

    This matches the lists given by Magnin.

  6. Now let us focus on the NLA \(L_{5, 3}\text{.}\)

    • Let us print out a basis of \(H^p(L_{5, 3},\mathbb{C})\) for \(p=1, 2, 3, 4\) with vectors stacked horizontally this time.

    • Then a basis of \(Z^p(L_{5, 3},\mathbb{C})\) for \(p=1, 2, 3, 4\text{.}\)

    • And finally a basis of \(B^p(L_{5, 3},\mathbb{C})\) for \(p=1, 2, 3, 4\text{.}\)
