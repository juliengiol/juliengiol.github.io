Here is Proposition 1.3.2 on page 8 in de Graaf's book.

Proposition 1.1.1.

Let \(A\) be an algebra with basis \(\{x_1,\ldots,x_n\}\text{.}\) Let the structure constants relative to this basis be \(c_{ij}^k\) for \(1\le i,j,k\le n\text{.}\) Then \(A\) is a Lie algebra if and only if the structure constants satisfy the following relations:

\begin{equation} c_{ii}^k=0\quad\text{and}\quad c_{ij}^k=-c_{ji}^k,\label{sc_antisymmetry}\tag{1.1.1} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \sum_{l=1}^nc_{il}^mc_{jk}^l+c_{jl}^mc_{ki}^l+c_{kl}^mc_{ij}^l=0\label{sc_jacobi}\tag{1.1.2} \end{equation}

for all \(1\le i,j,k,m\le n\text{.}\)
